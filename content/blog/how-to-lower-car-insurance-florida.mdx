### 2. Increase Your Deductible
If you are currently carrying a $250 or $500 deductible for comprehensive and collision coverage, you are paying a premium for it. By raising your deductible to $1,000, you take on slightly more risk in the event of an accident, but your monthly bill will plummet by 15% to 20%.

### 3. Bundle Your Policies
Do you rent an apartment or own a home? Buying your property insurance and your auto insurance from the exact same carrier is the easiest way to trigger a massive multi-line discount. Bundling can easily knock **$300 off your annual premium**.

### 4. Maintain a Clean Driving Record
In Florida, a single speeding ticket can increase your rates by 22%, while an at-fault accident can spike your bill by a staggering 45%. Driving defensively and avoiding claims is the best long-term strategy for cheap insurance. If you live in a high-risk area, read our specific guide on finding the [cheapest car insurance in Miami](/blog/cheapest-car-insurance-miami).

### 5. Use a Telematics (Tracking) Program
Almost every major provider in 2026 offers a telematics program (like Progressive's Snapshot or State Farm's Drive Safe & Save). By letting an app track your braking, speed, and mileage, safe drivers can earn a highly lucrative discount—sometimes up to 30%.

### 6. Improve Your Credit-Based Insurance Score
Florida law legally allows auto insurance companies to use your credit score to determine your rates. Statistically, drivers with poor credit file more claims. By paying down debt and boosting your credit score, you can transition into a better pricing tier and save upwards of **$400 annually**.

### 7. Remove Unnecessary Coverage on Older Cars
Are you driving a 2010 Honda Civic worth $3,000? Paying $800 a year for full collision and comprehensive coverage makes zero mathematical sense. If your car is older and paid off, consider dropping down to Florida's minimum liability limits.
