## Florida Minimum Car Insurance Requirements Explained (2026)

Data based on Florida insurance regulations and market analysis (2026).

**Florida minimum car insurance requirements in 2026 are:**
- **$10,000** Personal Injury Protection (PIP)
- **$10,000** Property Damage Liability (PDL)

Florida is a "no-fault" state. This means that if you get into an accident, your own insurance pays for your medical bills, regardless of who caused the crash. 

## Florida PIP and PDL Requirements (What You Legally Need)

According to Florida state insurance guidelines, to legally drive your car off the lot or register it in the state, you must carry these two specific types of coverage:

1. **PIP (Personal Injury Protection):** Covers 80% of your medical bills and 60% of lost wages if you are injured in a crash.
2. **PDL (Property Damage Liability):** Pays for damage you cause to someone else's vehicle or property.

### The Big Secret: Bodily Injury (BI) is NOT Required
Unlike almost every other state, standard Florida drivers are **NOT legally required** to carry Bodily Injury (BI) liability insurance. If you are on a very tight budget and just need to get your car legally on the road, dropping BI is the fastest way to get your policy down to [under $50 a month](/blog/cheap-car-insurance-under-50).

## ⚠️ Warning: Minimum Coverage Can Be Risky

While minimum insurance keeps you legal, it does NOT protect you financially in a serious accident.

If you cause a crash with injuries, or total a brand-new Mercedes, your $10,000 PDL limit will vanish instantly. You could be personally responsible for tens of thousands of dollars out of pocket. Minimum coverage is a short-term legal fix, not a long-term financial shield.
